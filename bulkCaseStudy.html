Bulk Spice Identifier

MY ROLE

UX/UI Design, Research, Mobile Design, Web Development, Illustration

THE CHALLENGE

I noticed that many of my friends, neighbors, and family shop for bulk spices at the local co-op grocery stores in Minneapolis as a way to save money, select exactly how much quantity of a certain spice they want, and to reduce packaging waste. When shopping in the bulk aisle, customers have to scoop out their spice and write the PLU number on the container. They are not required to write down the name of the spice. I had an experience once when I watched a friend throw away three containers of unnamed bulk spices because she did not know what they were, and then headed out the door to pick up some more bulk spices from the grocery store just so she could be sure she knew what the product was. This inspired me to create something that would help people identify their spices bought from co-op grocery stores so that they donâ€™t waste time and waste spices.

THE SOLUTION

I thought that creating an application to discover the name of spices from co-op grocery stores would help in saving time and reducing waste. However, I was not sure how prevalent this problem was and among who, so I decided to interview co-op shoppers.

THE PROCESS

I wanted to understand if the problem of needing to identify spices bought from co-op grocery stores was a common issue, and if so, what would help customers to not waste time and money by buying new spices and throwing away unidentified bulk spices. I interviewed 15 people who shop at co-op grocery stores in Minneapolis and was able to create three user personas. Based on my user interviews and resulting user profiles, I designed for simplicity and ease of use. I wanted to create an application that was easy to use and designed for when users are in the kitchen cooking. I researched similar applications using cometitive and comparative reviews to understand the landscape of applications like this. I then spent time sketching out the database structure and page structure on white board as you can see below. After this, I moved into creating wireframes both by sketching and in Axure. From there I began building the website.
